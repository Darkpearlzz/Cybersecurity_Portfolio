# Internal Security Audit: Botium Toys

## 📌 Objective & Scope

Conducted a comprehensive audit of Botium Toys entire internal security program to elevate their overall security posture. The assessment evaluated all existing IT assets including employee devices, internal networks, and systems—against a strict controls and compliance checklist to identify critical gaps and recommend best practices.

## 🛠️ Methodology

- **Framework:** NIST Cybersecurity Framework (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover)
- **Tools:** Vulnerability scanners, log analysis, policy reviews

## ⚠️ Executive Summary

**Overall Risk Score: 8/10 (High)**
An assessment revealed critical vulnerabilities in data protection, access control, and business continuity. While physical security and perimeter defenses (firewalls, AV) are functional, the lack of strict Identity and Access Management (IAM) and non-compliance with data privacy standards (e.g., PCI DSS) exposes the infrastructure to significant risk.

### 🛡️ Core Vulnerabilities Identified:

- **Unrestricted Access:** Universal employee access to unencrypted customer PII and credit card data.
- **Business Continuity Risks:** Complete absence of automated backups and a formal Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP).
- **Weak IAM Controls:** Inadequate password policies and lack of a centralized management system or MFA.
- **Limited Network Visibility:** Missing Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) and incomplete IT asset classification.
